Yale School of Arts's Home Webpage

This assignment requires us to choose a webpage to analyze and to redesign. I decided to choose The Yale School of Art's Home Webpage because of how unique it looks compared to other university websites in terms of organization of material, bold colors, and the collaborative aspect of it. Below is a screenshot of the original webpage.


Usability Problems

Learnability:


How easy is it for users to accomplish basic tasks the first time they encounter the design?
  • It is difficult for first time users. Users are here on this page primarily to find out a basic intro to Yale's School of Art, find/post upcoming events and opportunities, and/or editing the page.
  • Many different links that make it difficult to navigate: combination of links to other pages, to sections of the current page, and quick links.
  • Many calendar/event links scattered throughout the page and inconsistent ways of presentation.
  • Editing buttons are far apart from each other: the edit background and adding posts buttons are far from the edit page button.
  • There is a lot of "explanation" text. Users going through for the first time will need to spend a lot of time reading to learn how to use the site or know what is allowed. There are many similar links, but are slightly different so require careful reading of the description text.

Efficiency:


Once users have learned the design, how quickly can they perform tasks?
  • After some learning, users can figure out/better understand the link navigation.
  • The main issue is the positioning of features. The edit background and add post buttons are far down, and require the user to go through half the site to reach.
  • Again, there's a lot of "explanation" text. Repeated users will already know this information, but they have to scroll through a lot of it quickly.

Memorability:


When users return to the design after a period of not using it, how easily can they reestablish proficiency?
  • A unique thing about this site is that it encourages change and contribution from the community. This means that the site may change drastically after some period of time.
  • However if assuming the site is kept the way it was, it will be difficult to reestablish proficiency. They will probably have to again read carefully or flick through all the menus and links again.

Visual/Layout:


Visual and Layout problems.
  • All the information is condensed to the left portion of the page.
  • The colors make it difficult to read the text, very extreme contrast in close sections which makes it irritating on the eyes.
  • The boxes also have a gradient which makes it difficult to see.
  • Inconsistent font/heading sizes, making it more difficult to see the different in hierarchy.
  • Lots of text clutter.

Accessibility:


Analysis of Accessibility.
  • The WAVE tool produced alerts regarding headings and redundant links, which are an issue as pointed in the visual/layout problems.
  • It is pointed out that there are alt labels for some of the graphics.
  • There are ARIA labels present.
